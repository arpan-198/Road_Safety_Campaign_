OUR SOME SAFETY MEASURES


How did the measure progress?

  1. Development of methodology
  2. Training of personnel
  3. Selection of localities with the majority of road accidents
  4. Proposal of actions to improve safety, assessment of costs and benefits
  5. Measurements of traffic speed by static radars; Analysis of traffic flow characteristics based on measurements
  6. Data collection from local school and preschool facilities
  7. On-spot safety inspections by the local school and preschool facilities

What were the outcomes of the measure?

  1. Identify localities with the majority of accidents by periodic analysis
  2. Systematically eliminate safety deficits on the basis of detailed inspections
  3. Determine efficiency of realised measures by periodic analysis
  4. Remove the most serious safety deficits identified by the inspections
  5. Systematically find solutions from the most dangerous roads to less severe ones
  6. Prioritise detailed inspections for individual road sections
  7. Educate administration and maintenance personnel performing the periodic inspections

IMPORTANT SAFETY MEASURES TAKEN BY GOVERNMENT


A Multi Pronged Strategy

has been adopted by the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways, based on the 4 Es – Engineering, Enforcement, Education and Emergency Care for ensuring road safety. Here, better and safer road engineering, more effective legislation, improved vehicular safety standards, training of drivers, improved trauma care and generating public awareness are made.

Making engineering level innovations to improve road safety

The MORTH has established a Road Safety Cell (Engineering) (RSCE) in 2015 to exclusively work on Road Safety Engineering

Road Safety Bill

The MoRTH introduced the Motor Vehicle Bill 2016 in Parliament in August, 2016. The Bill addresses several road safety issues by providing for stiffer penalties, permitting electronic enforcement, improving fitness certification and licensing regime, statutory provisions for protection of good Samaritans and recognition of IT enabled enforcement systems etc.
